A rendering of the proposed park, courtesy of Springfield Parks Dept.

The Springfield City Council voted unanimously Thursday night to approve construction of Riverside Commons, a 14-acre public park along the eastern bank of the Springfield River. Funding for the $11.2M project comes from a mix of municipal bonds and a $4M state recreation grant.

"This is a generational investment in our shared spaces," said Mayor Theresa Ng during the vote. The park will feature a half-mile of riverfront walkway, two playgrounds, a community amphitheater, and a network of native-pollinator gardens.

Construction is slated to begin in early September, with phase one — the walkway and one of the playgrounds — projected to open by next summer. Total build-out is expected to take roughly two years.

Residents at the meeting expressed broad support but also raised concerns about parking and traffic on Maple Street. Council members said a follow-up traffic study would begin within 30 days.

The park replaces a derelict warehouse complex demolished last fall, part of a broader downtown revitalization effort that has added more than 200 housing units in the last three years.
