The mission was commanded by Neil Armstrong, with command module pilot Michael Collins and lunar module pilot Buzz Aldrin. Launched by a Saturn V rocket from Kennedy Space Center on Merritt Island, Florida, Apollo 11 was the fifth crewed mission of NASA's Apollo program.

Background

The Apollo program was conceived during the Eisenhower administration in early 1960, as a follow-on to Project Mercury. While Mercury capsules could only support one astronaut on a limited Earth-orbital mission, Apollo would carry three.

President John F. Kennedy's May 25, 1961 address to a joint session of Congress proposed a crewed lunar landing by the end of the decade, providing the impetus the agency needed.

Mission

The launch took place on schedule. After 1.5 Earth orbits, the S-IVB third-stage engine pushed the spacecraft onto its trajectory toward the Moon with the trans-lunar injection burn at 16:22:13 UTC.
